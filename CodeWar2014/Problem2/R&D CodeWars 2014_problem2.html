Question 2 - Restaurant at the end of the universe

The History of every major Galactic Civilization tends to pass through three distinct and recognizable phases, those of Survival, Inquiry and Sophistication, otherwise known as the How, Why, and Where phases.

For instance, the first phase is characterized by the question 'How can we eat?' the second by the question 'Why do we eat?' and the third by the question 'Where shall we have lunch?

We decide to go to the Restaurant at the End of Universe. On arriving there we find that the place is temporarily out of order. As we look around for the problem, we find that Chefs at The Restaurant at the End of Universe have extremely challenging jobs.

Apart from offering the great spectacle of the universe blowing itself up, it also offers a guarantee of maximizing your taste for a particular serving if you order the chef’s special.

Background

The restaurant offers two types of dishes, dishes like roasted potatoes, dumplings, hotdogs which are discrete dishes, i.e. you can eat them in integral numbers.

You can’t have them in fraction. The other types of dishes are like mashed potato, drinks, sauces etc. which are continuous dishes and you can have any arbitrary amount of them.

TastinessDiscrete = NΣn=1 (ti - (n-1)Δti)

TastinessContinuous = 0∫x (ti - xΔti)dx

Total tastiness of the dish served to you is the summation of total tastiness of its components. Also since anti-particles are not edible, total weight of a dish is the sum of total weight of its components.

Input

The input consists of a single test case which consists of multiple lines.

  1. The first line consists of two integers d and w (1 ≤ d ≤ 250; 1 ≤ w ≤ 10000). d is the number of components in the serving and w is the total weight of the serving in centigrams.

  2. After the first line, we have d lines where each line describes a component.

    a. If the input is of the form D witiΔti then it is a discrete dish where each item weighs wigrams with initial tastiness ti and decay of tastiness Δi

    b. If the input is of the form C ti Δti then it is a continuous dish with initial tastiness ti and decay of tastiness Δti

  3. The numbers 1 ≤ wi ≤ 10000; 0 ≤ ti ; Δti ≤ 10000

Output

We need to calculate the serving with maximum possible tastiness of a weight w. If it is impossible to make a meal of total weight exactly w based on available dishes, display impossible.

The output should contain the maximum tastiness of the serving.

Sample Input Sample Output
2 15 40.500000000
D 4 10 1
C 6 1
2 19
D 4 5 1
